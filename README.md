# 🍽️ Food Waste Analytics Dashboard

### **Short Description**
A visually engaging and analytical Power BI report designed to help a food group company reduce operational waste across its branches in Jeddah.  
It focuses on identifying patterns in production, sales, and spoilage — enabling data-driven decisions to minimize waste and improve inventory efficiency.

---

### 🧰 **Tech Stack**
- Power BI Desktop  
- Power Query  
- SQL Server (Views & Queries)  
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)  
- Excel  

---

### 🗂️ **Data Source**
The dataset simulates operational data from a food group’s branch network in Jeddah.  
It includes production, sales, wastage, and inventory-related information.  

Data was processed through SQL Views:
- **vw_WasteData** – tracks produced, sold, and wasted quantities  
- **vw_InventoryData** – provides category-level and supplier-level insights  

---

### 🌟 **Features & Highlights**

#### **1. Business Problem**
The food group was experiencing high levels of food wastage across multiple branches, leading to increased operational costs and inefficient inventory management.  
The challenge was to identify where, when, and why wastage was occurring.

#### **2. Goal of the Dashboard**
To analyze food wastage patterns, identify high-waste categories and locations, and uncover insights to optimize production and inventory planning.

#### **3. Walkthrough of Key Visuals**
- **KPI Cards** → Total Production, Total Waste, Waste %, and Estimated Waste Cost  
- **Category Analysis** → Waste by Food Category (Bakery, Dairy, Meat, etc.)  
- **Branch Comparison** → Waste distribution across different locations in Jeddah  
- **Time Trends** → Daily/Monthly waste trends to identify peak wastage periods  
- **Supplier Insights** → Impact of suppliers on product spoilage  

#### **4. Business Impact & Insights**
- Overall waste rate: **~22%**  
- **Perishable categories** (Bakery & Dairy) contributed the highest waste  
- Certain branches showed consistent overproduction patterns  
- Peak wastage observed during **low-demand periods (weekdays)**  
- Insights support better demand planning and supplier optimization strategies
